# Feature Fridays!

## Feature Fridays

Feature Fridays ran for approximately 6 months from April 2020 to October 2020, and added tons of new knowledge to Vector! Each entry here contains examples of the feature that was added, and in many cases there are many more questions to explore for that feature- especially Small Talk, Drink Recipes, and more! These features come at no added cost for those with a [Vector Membership](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/344-all-about-memberships). If you're new to Vector or want more ideas on how to interact with him, see ["Ways To Interact With Vector"](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/122-ways-to-interact-with-vector).

## #1: Drink Recipes

Vector now knows how to mix drinks! To be fair, he can't mix them for you, but he can definitely tell you what you'll need! Just think up your favorite drink, and try it out! Here are a few examples:

- "Hey Vector, I have a question. How do I make a mojito?"
- "Hey Vector, I have a question. How do you make a sea breeze?"
- "Hey Vector, question. How do I make an Aviation cocktail?"

## #2: The Periodic Table

Fear science homework no more! Vector can tell you about the Periodic Table and its elements. Here are a few examples:

- "Hey Vector, question. How many electrons are in Titanium?"
- "Hey Vector, question. How many protons are in Gallium?"
- "Hey Vector, I have a question. What is the symbol for Manganese?"

## #3: Music Theory

Vector can teach you about music and help you practice! Try a few of these:

- "Hey Vector, I have a question. How do you play a D chord major on piano?"
- "Hey Vector, I have a question. Show me how to play an A minor first inversion chord on the piano."
- "Hey Vector, question. Show me how to play a G minor chord on the piano."

**Quick Tip:**Try following up one of the above questions with another question about the inversions of your chosen chord!

## #21: Multi-Part Queries!

This week we enabled multi-part queries! Users are now able to issue follow up questions without having to back out and repeat previous commands!  An example would be as follows:
 "Hey Vector, I have a question, who is LeBron James?"  After it's answer, you can reply, "Hey, Vector, question, what high school did he attend?"
 In the past you would have to reference exactly who or what you wanted to know about, but now Vector will stay on topic!

## #23: Rock, Paper, Scissors & Music Search

Rock, Paper, Scissors, and Music Search for you to enjoy with your Vector.

##### To use Music Search, say (for example):

- "Hey Vector, I have a question: What are some Aerosmith songs?"
- "Hey Vector, question: "List the songs on the "Evolve" album by Imagine Dragons"

##### To play Rock, Paper, Scissors in a best out of five match with Vector, follow this process:

- Hey Vector, question. Play Rock, Paper, Scissors!
- Hey Vector, I have a question. (Say your option: Rock, Paper, or Scissors)

## #24: Guess The Number Mini-Game & Music Knowledge & Number Facts & Chinese Zodiac & Battleship

##### Chinese Zodiac:

- "Hey Vector, question. Find me the current Chinese Zodiac sign."

##### Battleship:

- To start, say: "Hey Vector, question. Start Battleship!
  - Vector will ask for a coordinate.
  - Valid coordinates are A-J, 1-10.
- To play, issue a coordinate: "Hey Vector, question. F4!"
  - Vector will tell you if you hit or miss.
