## What battery does the cube use?

- Alkaline N / E90 / LR1 1.5 Volt batteries
- Do not use A23 batteries - these will damage the Cube
- Do not use rechargeable batteries - these will damage the Cube

---

## How long does the battery last?

- Batteries come pre-installed in Cozmo's Cube
- Once the pull-tab is removed, the battery will start to lose charge
- The battery will last 20-40 hours of gameplay depending on how much Cozmo interacts with the Cube
- To test if the battery is working, reset the cube:

- unscrew the lid from the cube
- wait for 10 seconds
- screw the lid back on
- the cube should flash red>green>blue if the battery is fine

- In Settings > Cube Status, there will be a red Low Battery icon if the battery does not have enough power to sync / connect and needs to be replaced. Note: the low battery icon does not indicate battery voltage

---

## How do I change the battery?

1. Remove the cube’s screw using a cross head screwdriver and open the cube’s cover
2. Remove and [safely dispose](//support.digitaldreamlabs.com/article/129-product-disposal-information) of the old battery
3. Insert a new battery, positive end up
4. Replace the cover and screw the cover back down (not too tightly which can damage the cube)

## How does Cozmo interact with his cubes?

Cozmo interacts with his cubes on two levels:

- **Visually:** Cozmo can tell the difference between each cube by its markings. He can recognize the cube even without a battery

![](//d33v4339jhl8k0.cloudfront.net/docs/assets/5e3f0b1e2c7d3a7e9ae777f5/images/5e7d76e82c7d3a7e9ae9e339/img-2828-1585280656-1432163705.png)

- **Wirelessly:** Cozmo can connect wirelessly to each of his cubes if it is nearby. If a cube is too far away or the battery is low or dead, Cozmo may not be able to sync with it

## Indicators of cubes not working correctly

- One of all of the cubes LEDs do not light up but cube is detected in the “Settings > Cube status” menu
- Cozmo is unable to detect a cube
- Cozmo loses connection to a cube

---

## Troubleshooting

- If only one cube seems to be problematic, you may switch the batteries of a working cube to check if this is due to a dead battery
- Reset the cube:
  - unscrew the lid from the cube
  - wait for 10 seconds
  - screw the lid back on
  - the cube should flash red>green>blue if the battery is fine
- Move the cubes nearer to Cozmo
- Check the cubes are synced in “settings > cube status”
  - when connected, you should see a green check
  - if Cozmo is unable to sync to a cube, it will say, "not connected"
  - tap "auto connect to cubes" to re-sync the cubes, especially after resetting or changing the battery
